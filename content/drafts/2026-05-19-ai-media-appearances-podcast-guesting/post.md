Not every high-impact room is a keynote stage.

Sometimes the right format is a podcast, a broadcast segment, a trade-media interview, a produced video series, a panel, a moderated conversation, or an emcee role where the job is to keep the room human while the topic gets strange.

This is the media and appearances side of my AI work: making machine intelligence understandable, practical, creative, and culturally literate for real people.

## Broadcast And Public AI Explainers

The clearest mainstream proof point is CBC Radio Early Edition's `AI Sandbox with Kris Krug`.

The premise is simple: take the fast-moving AI conversation and make it safe enough for public testing. Not safe as in sanitized. Safe as in honest, curious, critical, and useful for listeners who are trying to understand what this technology means for their jobs, families, communities, and creative lives.

Related CBC/Indigenous AI coverage with Michael Running Wolf also belongs in this proof stack because it connects AI commentary to data sovereignty, ethical innovation, and community-centered technology.

## Produced Interview Series

The Horizons series is the polished produced-video version of this work.

Across short clips, the series covers AI as a human experience, AI in daily workflows, and next-generation innovation. It is useful producer proof because it shows I can bring the same ideas into a compact, high-production format without turning generic.

## Long-Form Podcast Conversations

The deeper podcast appearances show a wider range:

- Human Biography with Sharad Khare: curiosity, AI, creativity, art, photography, and ethics.
- Rachel Thexton Connects: AI, digital life, photography, technology, and heart.
- The Kurty D Show: photography, beauty, internet culture, AI, creative process, and putting work online.
- Teen2Life Experience: entrepreneurship, Future Proof Creatives, photography, and creative life.

Those conversations matter because a good podcast guest does more than deliver talking points. They bring stories, tensions, examples, and enough presence to make a long conversation feel alive.

## Trade And Event Interviews

The E-ChannelNews interview from ChannelNext Central adds another lane: AI for business audiences, managed service providers, practical adoption, and organizational readiness.

That is a different audience than an art school, a festival, or a public radio listener. The through-line is the same: make AI usable without pretending the risks are fake.

## Hosting And Owned Shows

Vancouver AI Pods and MOTLEYKRUG show the hosting side of the work.

These are not just content feeds. They are archives of a community learning in public: meetups, experiments, interviews, creative technology, AI ethics, Indigenous tech futurism, and the weird cultural edge where the future first becomes visible.

## Book Kris For A Podcast, Interview, Or Hosted Event

I can show up as a keynote speaker, workshop leader, podcast guest, broadcast explainer, panelist, moderator, host, or emcee.

The strongest topics are AI for creatives, responsible AI, human-centered tools, Vancouver and BC's AI ecosystem, creator rights, creative technology, community building, media futures, and the emotional reality of living through a technological shift this big.
