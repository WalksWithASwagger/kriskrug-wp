# Featured image brief

## Concept

**Title:** The Forensic Type Specimen

A dark editorial evidence board split between two eras of the same writer. On the left, a weathered 2005 blog page with imperfect serif type, a missing comma circled in red pencil, and a small contact sheet. On the right, a clean 2026 terminal window with sterile lines of AI-assisted prose and a gold score card. A thin thread connects both sides to the same fingerprint-like waveform.

## Art direction

- Wide editorial hero, 16:9 and safe to crop to 1.91:1.
- Charcoal-black background, warm paper white, oxidized gold, muted brick red.
- High-end magazine investigation, not cyberpunk and not a generic robot image.
- Tactile paper, terminal glass, red pencil marks, archival contact-sheet texture.
- No recognizable third-party product logos.
- No generated body copy. Leave headline space clean for a human-set title treatment.
- Mood: intimate, forensic, slightly funny, intellectually honest.

## Suggested title treatment

`A CHECKER CAN REMOVE THE SLOP.`

`IT CANNOT ADD A PERSON.`

Set by a human after image generation in a high-contrast serif plus small monospaced label.

## Alt text

An editorial collage pairs a weathered 2005 blog page with a clean 2026 terminal, connected by the same waveform and marked with red forensic annotations.
