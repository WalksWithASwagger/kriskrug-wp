Welcome to the age of **astroturfed altruism**, where “community” is a marketing ploy, and “volunteering” is the sleek rebrand of free labor. At first glance, the AI Summit Vancouver 2025 Steering Committee call reads like a dream: co-create Canada’s largest grassroots AI conference, join a network of tech dreamers, and bask in resume-worthy glory. But peel back the pastel language and you’ll find a darker subtext: *this isn’t community building, it’s labor extraction under the guise of empowerment*.

## The Anatomy of a Hustle

Let’s decode the template. A nonprofit waves the banner of inclusivity, plugs into trendy themes like “democratizing AI,” and asks for skilled volunteers to fill roles like **Web Developer**, **Project Manager**, and **Digital Marketing Lead**. These aren’t “help out for a few hours” gigs, they’re the operational spine of any for-profit tech event.

But instead of offering wages, the “benefits” include:

A “Certificate of Recognition”Public shout-outs“Portfolio credibility”Networking (aka the hope of paid work... someday)

This isn’t community-driven. It’s **corporate cosplay**, performed on the backs of hopeful creatives and underemployed professionals hungry for relevance in a precarious economy.

## The Legal Fine Print: It&#x27;s Not Just Shady, It&#x27;s Potentially Illegal

Under B.C.’s **Employment Standards Act**, the second you assign deadlines, enforce weekly hour quotas, and start managing deliverables, your “volunteer” magically transforms into an **employee**. Whether you meant to or not, you’re on the hook for:

Minimum wageOvertimeVacation payPotential penalties

There’s no clause that says “but we’re a nonprofit with good vibes.” Intention doesn’t override labor law. Exploitation with a smiley face is still exploitation.

## Community as Camouflage

This isn’t new. It’s part of a broader trend where the word *community* has been hijacked by institutions seeking free labor, unpaid advocacy, and organic clout, without ever redistributing value. It’s **community-washing**, the nonprofit sector’s answer to greenwashing. Aesthetic inclusion without economic justice.

The vibe is DIY. The power dynamic is pure corporate.

The org gets the labor. You get a badge on LinkedIn.

Ask yourself: if this work is essential, strategic, and high-skill, why isn’t it paid?

## Who Really Benefits?

Not the volunteers. Not the attendees. Not the broader “AI community.”

The real beneficiary is the **entity that centralizes prestige, power, and access**, and pays for none of the fuel driving the engine. It’s a model of **extractive inclusion**: opening the gates just wide enough to pull people in, but never so wide that value flows both ways.

It’s the **creator economy without the creator equity**.

It’s the **hackathon that hacks the hacker**.

It’s **techno-utopia built on unpaid infrastructure**.

## Stop Falling for the Glow-Up

The next time a shiny new summit, conference, or community-led initiative calls for your passion but not your invoice, ask:

Who owns this platform?Who is profiting from this labor?Would this be acceptable in any other industry?

Because you are not a “volunteer.” You are labor. And your labor is valuable.

## The Path Forward: From Tokenism to True Community

Real community work doesn’t hide behind NDAs and unpaid deliverables. It’s built on **mutual aid, transparent governance, and shared ownership**. If we want AI, art, and activism to evolve beyond extractive logics, we must abandon these hollow structures dressed in communal drag.

We need more **platform co-ops**, not pretend “steering committees.”

More **paid micro-leads**, not unpaid digital janitors.

More **creator equity**, less clout-flavored labor laundering.

If your organization can’t afford to pay people, it can’t afford to run a summit.

### Stop calling it community when it’s just capitalism without the courtesy of a paycheck.

**Join the resistance. Name the game. Rewrite the source code.**
