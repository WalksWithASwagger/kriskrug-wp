Most of my public AI work happens in rooms: keynotes, workshops, meetups, panels, classrooms, and strange little experiments where people are trying to make sense of the future together.

The Horizons series is useful proof of a different mode.

It is produced media. Short, polished, topic-driven, and designed for people who want a clear guest who can make AI feel human without sanding off the weirdness.

That matters for the speaking page because the job is not only to show that I can hold a stage. It is also to show producers, hosts, moderators, and conference teams that I can be dropped into a high-production format and make the ideas land.

## AI As A Human Experience

The first clip frames AI as more than automation.

The interesting question is not whether machines can make us faster. They can. The deeper question is what happens when AI becomes part of how we think, decide, remember, imagine, and interact with our own creative process.

That is where the conversation gets more human, and much more complicated.

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EBGdM6T9Fr8

## AI In Daily Work And Life

The second clip is more practical.

AI can be a personal assistant, a creative partner, a journaling system, an advisory board, and a second brain for decision-making. That does not mean handing over your life to a chatbot. It means designing tools that support reflection, synthesis, and better choices.

The real skill is not "using AI." It is knowing which parts of your work and life deserve a system around them.

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tfXkDhlqnrE

## Next-Generation Innovation

The third clip looks at young builders and creative technologists.

This is the part of the AI story I do not want people to miss: the future is not only being built by big labs and enterprise vendors. It is also being shaped by students, artists, hackers, accessibility builders, and weirdly ambitious young people who now have tools that can amplify a good idea quickly.

That is why AI literacy has to be creative literacy too.

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pfecN8_1boA

## Why This Belongs In The Speaking Stack

Keynotes, workshops, podcasts, interviews, panels, and produced series are all different rooms.

The best guests can adapt without becoming generic. They can speak to executives, creatives, students, technical teams, and curious civilians without losing the human thread.

That is the lane I am building toward: AI that is practical, ethical, creative, emotionally honest, and useful to real people.
