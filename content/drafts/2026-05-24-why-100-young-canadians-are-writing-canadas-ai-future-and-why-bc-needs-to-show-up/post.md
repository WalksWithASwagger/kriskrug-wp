While Alberta throws billions at data centers and Ottawa writes policy in committee rooms, something different is happening across four Canadian cities.

Gen(Z)AI is putting 100 young Canadians, ages 17 to 23, in front of AI experts, policy makers, and each other to build consensus on how this country should handle artificial intelligence. Not performative consultation. Not youth advisory boards that get thanked and ignored. Actual policy development that feeds into federal legislative frameworks.

**The Setup:**

Four regional forums: Toronto, Montreal, Vancouver, Halifax100 youth in person, thousands participating digitallySix-month process leading to consensus recommendationsOrganized by Democracy over Tech + McGill&#x27;s Centre for Media, Technology and DemocracyBacked by Mila and Make.orgVancouver forum: February 20–22, 2025

**Why This Matters:**

Most youth engagement is bullshit. Kids get paraded in front of cameras, asked their feelings, thanked for their input, then adults do whatever they were going to do anyway.

This is different. These forums use deliberative democracy methods, the kind that actually move policy. Participants learn from experts, debate with peers, build consensus positions that have weight because they represent cross-regional, demographically representative youth perspectives.

Canada&#x27;s ramping up AI strategy. Federal government is listening (or claims to be). This assembly gets young voices into that process before the lobbying money drowns them out.

**The BC Angle:**

Our forum hits February. We have five youth members in BC + AI. We need way more than that if this province is going to punch above its weight in national AI conversations.

BC has clean energy advantages Alberta can&#x27;t match. We have Indigenous data sovereignty frameworks other provinces are still figuring out. We have grassroots AI communities that build instead of just talk. Our youth need to be loud in these forums.

**What You Can Do:**

If you&#x27;re 17–23 and Canadian, apply: [mediatechdemocracy.com/gen-z-ai](https://www.mediatechdemocracy.com/gen-z-ai)

Applications close October 17. Random selection after demographic sorting. Transportation stipends available.

If you know youth who should be there, especially Indigenous youth, youth from smaller BC communities, youth building things, send them the link.

If you&#x27;re not eligible but give a shit about who shapes AI policy, amplify this. The alternative is letting the usual suspects write the rules while young people watch from the sidelines.

**Meet the Organizer:**

Fergus Linley-Mota is speaking at Vancouver AI Community Meetup on December 13th at HR MacMillan Space Centre. He&#x27;ll break down the Gen(Z)AI model, share what&#x27;s coming out of the first forums, and explain how BC participants can make noise in February.

Also: Ruby Singh might be performing that night. Keep that quiet.

**The Real Question:**

We keep saying AI is reshaping everything. We keep saying young people will inherit whatever systems we build now. Do we actually mean it?

Gen(Z)AI says yes. BC better show up.

Youth at the wheel: Gen(Z)AI is building Canada’s first youth citizens’ assembly on AI

**Post**

Canada’s AI rules are getting written. Gen(Z)AI makes sure young people don’t just get a token seat, they help steer. The Centre for Media, Technology &amp; Democracy at McGill, SFU’s Dialogue on Technology (DoT), and Mila are convening 100 Canadians (17–23) across four city forums, Toronto, Montréal, Vancouver, and Halifax, to learn from experts, deliberate, and publish consensus policy recommendations. Applications are national, selection is randomized for demographic representation, and access is bilingual with travel support. A final plenary in Ottawa brings youth proposals to policymakers. Vancouver’s forum runs **Feb 20–22** with a focus on **AI &amp; Data Privacy**. [Media, Tech &amp; Democracy Center](https://www.mediatechdemocracy.com/gen-z-ai)

Why we care in BC: our AI scene works because it’s grassroots, cross-disciplinary, and values-driven. Putting youth in the loop keeps that ecosystem honest and future-proof, more community, less extractive hype.

Partners are building a participatory pipeline, not just a roadshow. Make.org is supporting the digital engagement layer so thousands more youth can weigh in, while DoT brings BC’s dialogue muscle from Wosk Centre into a national frame. [Make.org+2Make.org+2](https://about.make.org/articles-en/make-org-partners-on-canadian-gen-z-ai-youth-assembly-on-ai?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

**What they’ll tackle**

**AI &amp; Data Privacy (Vancouver, Feb 20–22)**, what data should be collected, and what must be protected?**AI &amp; Information Integrity (Montréal)**, truth, trust, and synthetic media.**AI &amp; Age Assurance (Halifax)**, safety without surveillance creep.**AI &amp; Chatbots (Toronto)**, safe use across school, work, and life. [Media, Tech &amp; Democracy Center](https://www.mediatechdemocracy.com/gen-z-ai)

This lines up with our stance: double-down on community, evolve education, and invest in infrastructure that serves people, especially new entrants whose first “AI job” will look nothing like yesterday’s titles. Youth deliberation is how we design that on purpose.
