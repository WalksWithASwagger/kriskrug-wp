[*Kris Krüg*](https://kriskrug.co/)*, Executive Director, *[*BC + AI Ecosystem Association*](https://bc-ai.ca/)

Last October, I watched a room full of 250 people at the Vancouver Planetarium lean forward when our speaker asked: &quot;How many of you are deploying AI systems at work right now?&quot; Nearly every hand went up.

Then he asked: &quot;How many of you have a governance framework for how you&#x27;re doing it?&quot; 

The hands dropped. Maybe a dozen stayed raised.

That gap, between deployment speed and ethical practice, is why we built our new [Responsible AI Professional Certification](https://bc-ai.ca/certification/responsible-ai-professional/) program.

## The Problem We&#x27;re Trying to Solve

Every organization is rolling out AI. Fast. The pressure to ship, to automate, to not fall behind, it&#x27;s real. I get it. I&#x27;ve been building technology for 25 years. I helped build the world&#x27;s first Drupal development company. I created [Dead.net](http://dead.net/) for the Grateful Dead. I wrote books about BitTorrent and iPhone photography when those were emerging technologies.

I&#x27;ve seen this pattern before: new tech arrives, everyone scrambles to adopt it, and the ethics conversation happens later. Usually after something goes wrong.

With AI, we can&#x27;t afford &quot;later.&quot;

And here&#x27;s what I&#x27;ve learned running the Vancouver AI Meetup for the past two years: most people aren&#x27;t trying to do harm. They&#x27;re just moving fast and nobody gave them the frameworks to do it responsibly.

## Why BC + AI Built This

When we started the Vancouver AI Meetup in 2024, we didn&#x27;t know it would grow from 80 people in my studio to 250+ monthly attendees at the Space Centre. We didn&#x27;t know we&#x27;d end up with 850+ Discord members, 99+ paying members, and seven Special Interest Groups covering everything from creative AI to enterprise governance.

What we did know was that our community kept asking the same questions:

*&quot;How do I evaluate whether this AI system should be deployed?&quot;*

*&quot;What frameworks actually work for assessing risk?&quot;*

*&quot;Who&#x27;s teaching this stuff in a way that&#x27;s practical, not just academic?&quot;*

The certification programs that existed were either too theoretical (great for research papers, useless for Tuesday&#x27;s deployment decision) or too narrow (focused on one jurisdiction&#x27;s regulations, one company&#x27;s products, one technical domain).

We wanted something different. We wanted practical frameworks from multiple perspectives like UNESCO, OECD, NIST, and IEEE, applied to real scenarios. We wanted cohort-based learning where people wrestle with these questions together. We wanted participants to leave with actual artifacts they&#x27;d use, not just a certificate.

So we built it. RAP, the Responsible AI Professional, is BC + AI&#x27;s first certification program, developed in collaboration with TheUpgrade.

## What You&#x27;ll Actually Learn

RAP is four weeks, 90 minutes live each week, with pre-work and practical exercises. It&#x27;s not attendance-based. You have to pass quizzes and complete artifacts. Here&#x27;s the structure:

Each week you build an artifact: Personal AI Inventory, Ethics Assessment, Deployment Checklist, Ethics Impact Assessment. 

By the end, these become your custom Ethics Practice Assistant, a GPT trained on your work that knows how you think about these issues.

## Who&#x27;s Teaching This

I&#x27;m the program lead. I bring the community perspective, I&#x27;ve spent two years listening to what people actually struggle with, what questions keep coming up, what gaps exist between the theory and practice.

**Martin Lopatka** developed the curriculum. PhD in Forensic Statistics, Master&#x27;s in AI, Mozilla alumni with production ML systems experience. He knows responsible AI assessment frameworks cold. He&#x27;s volunteering his expertise because this work matters to him.

**Sarah Downey** is our instructor focused on governance facilitation. She&#x27;s been in nonprofit leadership for 20+ years and now helps mission-driven organizations adopt AI responsibly. Her perspective grounds everything we do in values-centered practice.

What unites us: we believe this work requires both technical depth and human understanding. You can&#x27;t do responsible AI with just frameworks. You can&#x27;t do it with just good intentions either.

## Who This Is For

RAP is built for three audiences:

**Leaders and executives** who are overseeing AI deployments and need governance frameworks that actually work. Not policy documents that sit in a drawer. Real tools for real decisions.

**Career transitioners** who have upskilling funds and want to differentiate themselves. If you&#x27;re pivoting into AI governance or want to be the person your organization turns to when these questions come up, this is for you.

**People who want to build a practice** rather than just get a credential. You&#x27;re not here to check a box. You&#x27;re here to walk away with frameworks, artifacts, and tools you&#x27;ll actually use next week.

What you don&#x27;t need: technical AI experience. We&#x27;ll explain how the systems work. But this isn&#x27;t a programming course. If you&#x27;re making decisions about AI, budgeting, deploying, governing, you&#x27;re qualified.

## The Bigger Picture

Here&#x27;s why this matters beyond the individual credential:

At BC + AI, we&#x27;ve built something unusual. We open every monthly meetup with Indigenous ceremony, not as acknowledgment theater, but as structural grounding. We&#x27;re on the unceded territories of the Musqueam, Squamish, and Tsleil-Waututh peoples, and we try to act like it.

We&#x27;ve turned down $10K sponsors whose practices conflicted with our values. When your community has principles, you have to be willing to live by them.

RAP is an extension of that philosophy. We&#x27;re not training people to check compliance boxes. We&#x27;re building practitioners who understand that technology choices are human choices, that AI systems encode values, that &quot;neutral&quot; is a position too.

The organizations that will lead in the AI era aren&#x27;t the ones with the fastest models. They&#x27;re the ones with people who know how to ask: *Should we build this? Who gets hurt if we get it wrong? What oversight do we need? How do we maintain human agency?*

That&#x27;s what we&#x27;re teaching.
