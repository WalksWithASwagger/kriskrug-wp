By Kris Krüg

The World Doesn’t Need More Influencers. It Needs Insurgents.

Everyone’s hustling for digital relevance: posting “authenticity,” peddling the latest gadget, shilling wellness hacks like snake oil for the soul. But here’s the heresy, none of that matters if it’s not making the world weirder, wilder, and more awake.

This is the story of building a career, a community, and a way of being that refuses to fit into any branded, beige box. This is how I became a creative insurgent, first in art, then in tech, now at the hotwired edge of AI, community organizing, and what I call “culture hacking.” My job isn’t to blend in, it’s to crack the system open and let something new grow in the gaps.

BEGINNING: ROOTS, REBELLION, AND WHY I NEVER FELT LIKE AN “ARTIST”

My first act of rebellion was skipping the line. While most people chase credentials, I started a web agency before Zuckerberg had dorm-room posters, ran social media campaigns before Instagram was a glint in a VC’s eye, and shot National Geographic spreads before I could legally rent a car. I built communities around open-source before “community” was a marketing word.

Every pivot came from a refusal to play the same tired game:

, When I saw that galleries were gatekeepers, I shot my own stories and published them Creative Commons.

, When tech felt sterile, I started mixing code with protest, film festivals with street photography, art collectives with hackathons.

, When the “scene” turned into a feedback loop of self-congratulation, I opened it up, inviting the misfits, the margin-walkers, and the hungry kids who had ideas that scared the boomers.

I don’t do this for “followers.” I do it because boredom is a death sentence, and conformity is a virus.

THE VANCOUVER EXPERIMENT: HOW TO BUILD A MOVEMENT WITH DUCT TAPE AND VISION

Landing in Vancouver, I started a community from nothing but nerve and a garage-sale PA. The Vancouver AI Community isn’t just another meet-and-greet for data bros and startup founders, it’s a living, breathing experiment in open-source culture. Our events are less TED Talk, more underground show: artists riff with engineers, coders jam with Indigenous leaders, and every meeting is a laboratory for what comes next.

We run monthly deep dives. Not “panels,” not “firesides”, real talks, demos, performances. No one leaves without a new contact, a new idea, or at least the sense that something vital just happened.

It’s work. Real work. It’s late-night transcription sessions, chasing down new voices, running Discord servers, and fielding calls from lost kids who just landed in town looking for a tribe. But if you want to build something that lasts, you don’t wait for an invitation. You draw your own map and dare people to walk with you.

AI AS A TOOL, NOT A TRAP: HOW TO SURF THE BLOOD BATH

Let’s talk about the elephant eating the room: generative AI. You’re watching it, careers vaporized, creative jobs reshuffled, automation coming for the so-called “safe” knowledge workers. Customer service, coding, design, AI’s not on the horizon, it’s at the table, eating your lunch.

But here’s the pivot no one wants to talk about: If you’re just cranking out “correct answers,” you’re in the firing line. If your job is paint-by-numbers, the machine’s coming for your seat. The antidote? Stop being predictable. Make your work more idiosyncratic, more human, more “you” than a thousand lines of code could ever fake.

I’m not hand-wringing about job loss. I’m giving people the tools to build their own exoskeletons, to become cyborgs by choice, not by accident. That’s what The Upgrade AI">TheUpgrade.ai is for: hands-on training, not hype. We teach creatives, writers, makers how to build knowledge bases, custom AI agents, workflows that multiply their reach, not reduce their role to a checkbox. Every cohort I run, I see people go from “AI is going to replace me” to “AI is my sidekick, my ghost writer, my creative amplifier.”

If you’re not shaping the tools, the tools will shape you.

FROM INDIGENOUS TECH TO FUTURE-PROOFED FESTIVALS: COLLABORATION OR EXTINCTION

I don’t work alone. I build coalitions, even when it’s messy. With the Indigenomics Institute, I help develop AI and digital tools that reflect Indigenous economic sovereignty, using tech to amplify, not erase, culture. With FATALE Festival, I co-create spaces where the so-called “outsiders” set the agenda.

I lead, but I don’t gatekeep. The future belongs to the ones who can collaborate without diluting their vision. That means learning from the land, centering those left out of the last round of tech utopianism, and never letting a corporate lobbyist tell you what’s “innovative.”

ART, CHAOS, AND THE PRODUCTIVITY LIE

People ask about my process like it’s some linear pipeline. Truth: it’s chaos gardening. I cultivate mess, follow curiosity like a bloodhound, and turn failed experiments into compost for the next thing.

My tools are a mashup of analog and digital, DSLRs, open-source code, Discords, field notebooks, wild dreams, and a refusal to let the algorithm flatten my voice.

My mantra: “Ship it, then share the source.”

My warning: If your workflow can’t survive in public, it’s not worth building.

WHAT’S NEXT: FROM SIGNAL TO UPRISING

I’m not here to sell “success.” I’m here to show what’s possible when you treat your work like a living, evolving organism. I’m not afraid to burn down a legacy or throw gasoline on a new idea.

My legacy?

, Give away more than I take.

, Build communities that outlast me.

, Stay anti-fragile.

, Keep the code open and the vision bigger than any brand.

If you’re reading this looking for a blueprint, you missed the point. There is no map for the edge. You build it as you go.

CHALLENGER IDEA

The influencer age is dying. The age of creative insurgency is just getting started.

Here’s my question for you:

What are you building that can’t be co-opted, captured, or killed?

What community are you willing to fight for when the cameras are off?

What will you leave behind, another self-portrait, or a toolkit for the next generation?

Your move.
