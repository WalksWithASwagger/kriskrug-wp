# Developing an AI Mindset

## From tool panic to useful practice

AI changes too fast for a tidy software rollout. I would rather help your crew build a durable posture: stay curious, test the tools against real work, learn together, and keep human judgment visible.

Developing an AI Mindset is a practical keynote or workshop for organizations, schools, associations, nonprofits, and creative crews that need shared language before they need another stack of software.

## The software will change. The posture is the durable bit.

The original framework put technical skills beside human ones.

On the technical side: prompting, workflow design, organization, compositing, and model training where it is genuinely useful.

On the human side: curiosity, adaptability, discernment, collaborative learning, and critical thinking.

The second list matters more than ever. A clever prompt can get you an answer. Discernment helps you decide if the answer deserves a place in your work.

## What we work through

### Name the real job

Start with a task, decision, or creative block that already exists. A useful experiment begins with the work in front of you, not a generic tour of whatever launched this week.

### Test small and keep receipts

Pick one or two experiments. Track what helped, what broke, what felt strange, and where a person needed to step back in. Progress comes from practice, not performance.

### Keep judgment in the loop

AI can help with research, drafts, analysis, and creative play. People still own the context, values, consequences, and final call. We make those boundaries visible instead of hoping everybody silently shares them.

### Learn with other people

The people in the room do half the work. They bring the friction, examples, and questions that no generic demo can know. Shared practice turns isolated experiments into useful organizational knowledge.

### Choose the next move

Leave with one concrete action for the next week: a workflow to test, a question to investigate, a boundary to document, or a conversation your crew has been avoiding.

## A both/and frame for AI

AI can be useful, weird, and creatively electric. It also raises hard questions about privacy, bias, authorship, labour, trust, and what we refuse to automate.

Forced optimism is useless. So is pretending the tools are going away. The practical move is to hold curiosity and critique at the same time, then make choices with your eyes open.

This session gives a room a way to name both sides, try something real, and keep agency with the people doing the work.

## Who this is for

- Leaders who need plain language before they make AI decisions.
- Creative and communications crews testing new workflows without surrendering taste.
- Educators and schools helping people build practical AI literacy.
- Associations and nonprofits trying to turn scattered experiments into shared practice.
- Curious newcomers who want a humane first step without hype theatre.

No technical background is required. Bring a real question and a willingness to experiment.

## From the original talk to the work now

I delivered Developing an AI Mindset at Innovate West in 2024. The public deck framed the work around curiosity, adaptability, lifelong learning, technical practice, and the human skills needed to work through change. [See the original Innovate West deck](https://innovatewest.tech/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/Developing-an-AI-Mindset.pdf).

The wider practice now has a few distinct doors:

- [Punk Rock AI](https://www.punkrockai.com/) is the culture-first lane for creative agency, taste, and making with the tools without becoming obedient to them.
- [Both Hands Full](https://www.bothhandsfull.com/) holds critique and possibility together for artists, filmmakers, and people working through synthetic media.
- [Responsible AI Professional](https://bc-ai.ca/certification/responsible-ai-professional) is the deeper governance lane for people accountable for deployment decisions.

Developing an AI Mindset is the front door: shared language, useful experiments, human judgment, and a practical next move.
