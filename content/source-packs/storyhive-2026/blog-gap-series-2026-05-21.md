## Recommended Series

### 1. The 75% Rule: Automate The Art-Adjacent, Protect The Art

**Working slug:** `the-75-percent-rule-ai-art-adjacent-work`
**Format:** evergreen field manual
**Audience:** artists, photographers, filmmakers, designers, creative pros
**Promise:** AI should attack the admin sludge around art before it touches the sacred center.

**Why this fills a gap:** The live blog has strong "taste" and "workflow" posts, but does not yet have this compact operating rule. It is practical, memorable, and teachable.

**Core argument:**

- Most artists do not spend most of their time making the art.
- They spend it tagging, titling, archiving, invoicing, scheduling, writing captions, building decks, updating CRMs, extracting clips, and explaining the work.
- That is the first automation target.
- The win is not replacing the photographer in the room. The win is making the archive searchable so the real people, real bodies, real events, and real stories become easier to find and honor.

**Voice notes:** funny, direct, no hand-wringing. "Do not send a robot to do a human's blessing. Send it to do your damn filenames."

**Internal links:** `Both Hands Full`, `Make Culture, Not Content`, `Why Judgment Beats "Creativity"`, `AI Training for Media, PR, and Creative Professionals`.

**CTA:** TheUpgrade.ai creative professionals or speaking/workshop inquiry.

**Draft risk:** Low. Strong first post.

### 2. I Won't Fake The People Who Showed Up

**Working slug:** `i-wont-fake-the-people-who-showed-up`
**Format:** documentary ethics manifesto
**Audience:** photographers, filmmakers, event producers, AI-curious orgs
**Promise:** In the synthetic age, documentary presence becomes more valuable, not less.

**Why this fills a gap:** The blog has plenty on AI image generation and art. It needs a clean line around documentary truth: no generated substitute for a person who was actually there.

**Core argument:**

- AI can help plan a shoot, find patterns in an archive, draft captions, and process proof packs.
- AI should not replace the portrait of a community member, the documentation of a gathering, or the witness function of a photographer in the room.
- Documentary work is not just "image output." It is relationship, consent, attention, and being there when the room changes.

**Voice notes:** deep wisdom with backbone. This should feel like a grown-up photographer talking to the AI era from inside the scar tissue of the internet.

**Internal links:** `Punk Rock AI`, `Both Hands Full`, `Autolume/Post-Photographic Cybernetic Portraiture`, relevant Work/Speaking pages.

**CTA:** documentary/event photography and keynote booking.

**Draft risk:** Low to medium. Avoid overclaiming universal rules; frame as KK's practice.

### 3. Voice Clones Are Trust Dynamite

**Working slug:** `voice-clones-are-trust-dynamite`
**Format:** personal ethics essay
**Audience:** responsible AI practitioners, artists, educators, families, media people
**Promise:** The closer AI gets to our intimate signals, the slower and clearer we need to move.

**Why this fills a gap:** The site has older deepfake/voice posts, but the interview has a sharper lived lesson: disclosure is not a checkbox when the synthetic artifact touches family, romance, grief, memory, or identity.

**Core argument:**

- Voice is not just audio. It is relationship.
- A fake voice sent as a surprise can land as wonder for the maker and violation for the receiver.
- Consent needs to happen before the emotional reveal, not after.
- "Build in public" is not just marketing. It is how people can course-correct before trust gets damaged.

**Use with care:** The mom story is powerful but sensitive. The dating/music story should probably be held back or anonymized heavily unless KK explicitly approves it.

**Voice notes:** tender, accountable, no self-flagellation, no tech panic. "I learned this one the expensive way."

**Internal links:** `Applied Ethical AI: RAP`, `Love in the Time of AI`, `Digital Self & the Era of AI`, RAP certification page.

**CTA:** RAP / responsible AI workshops.

**Draft risk:** High. Needs human review before writing.

### 4. Artists Are Not Creative Professionals, Except When They Are

**Working slug:** `artists-creative-professionals-ai`
**Format:** thought leadership explainer
**Audience:** arts orgs, schools, studios, funders, policy people
**Promise:** If we flatten "artist" and "creative professional," we misunderstand both the labor crisis and the meaning crisis.

**Why this fills a gap:** The interview gave a clean distinction that the blog has not packaged yet.

**Core argument:**

- Creative professions are being transformed by AI because many paid tasks are predictable and automatable.
- Art matters more, not less, because meaning, beauty, truth, embodiment, and ritual are not interchangeable with production throughput.
- The same human may wear both hats in the same day, which is why the conversation feels so confusing.
- Good AI strategy distinguishes which hat is on before prescribing tools.

**Voice notes:** sassy taxonomy. "One minute you're invoicing a client, the next minute you're asking what truth and beauty mean after the machines eat the jingles."

**Internal links:** `Both Hands Full`, `Make Culture, Not Content`, `AI Training for Media, PR, and Creative Professionals`, `Spa at the End of Time`.

**CTA:** art-school/keynote booking.

**Draft risk:** Medium. Avoid sounding dismissive of professional craft.

### 5. Speak It Into Existence

**Working slug:** `speak-it-into-existence-ai-voice-first-workflows`
**Format:** practical workflow post
**Audience:** neurodivergent creatives, founders, writers, solo operators, workshop buyers
**Promise:** Voice-first AI is not just faster typing. It changes what part of you gets into the system.

**Why this fills a gap:** Existing posts mention agents and knowledge bases, but the interview's body-based insight is fresher: fingers filter the head and heart.

**Core argument:**

- Typing is not neutral.
- Speaking carries rhythm, slang, hesitation, urgency, and worldview.
- Voice-first capture improves AI output because it gives the model more of the real person.
- Agentic workflows are not just for coders; code is how digital things come alive, but the creative direction can start in plain speech.

**Voice notes:** useful and liberating. Less "productivity hack," more "take the cork out of your brain."

**Internal links:** `AI Ate the Future`, `How to Build an AI Second Brain`, `Make Culture, Not Content`.

**CTA:** creative AI workflow workshop.

**Draft risk:** Low. Strong evergreen.

### 9. Ask "Who Am I?" Then Distrust The Answer

**Working slug:** `ai-mirror-who-am-i`
**Format:** deep wisdom essay with a practical exercise
**Audience:** creatives with archives, coaches, educators, spiritual-but-practical readers
**Promise:** AI can help reveal your creative patterns, but it does not get the final word on your identity.

**Why this fills a gap:** The blog has "AI as mirror," but not this careful combination of archive analysis, meditation practice, critical play, and self-correction.

**Core argument:**

- A creative archive can become a mirror: photos, talks, classes, journals, drafts.
- The AI will see patterns. Some will be useful. Some will be wrong.
- The value is in the conversation: "you missed this," "add my meditation journals," "compare this to my actual values."
- Do not believe everything you think. Do not believe everything the AI thinks either.

**Voice notes:** deep wisdom, playful, non-dogmatic.

**Internal links:** `Make Culture, Not Content`, `How to Build an AI Second Brain`, `Punk Rock AI`, `The KK Worldview`.

### 10. What I Won't Let AI Do

**Working slug:** `what-i-wont-let-ai-do`
**Format:** boundaries essay
**Audience:** everyone who asks "what is sacred now?"
**Promise:** The best way to use AI is to know what stays off limits no matter how good the machine gets.

**Why this fills a gap:** The interview ends with a sacred human-connection frame. The blog has pieces of it, but not the simple personal boundary list.

**Core argument:**

- I will use AI to organize, prototype, analyze, remix, and help me see.
- I will not outsource presence, meditation, witness, intimacy, consent, or the human act of showing up for people.
- The more synthetic the world gets, the more deliberate we need to be about embodied connection.
- The life raft is relationship.

**Voice notes:** clean, moving, not melodramatic. This is the final piece in the first series wave.

**Internal links:** `Both Hands Full`, `I Won't Fake The People Who Showed Up`, `Voice Clones Are Trust Dynamite`, `Rooms Are The New Media`.

**CTA:** newsletter subscribe / speaking.

**Draft risk:** Medium. Needs restraint to avoid sounding anti-tool.
