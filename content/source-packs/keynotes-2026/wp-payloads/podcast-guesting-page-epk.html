What I bring to the mic

I can explain AI in plain language, but I am not a plain-language-only guest. The good stuff lives in the tension: creator rights, community trust, business adoption, synthetic media, public-interest AI, Indigenous data sovereignty, archive memory, weird creative tools, and the emotional reality of learning in public while the ground is moving.

I am useful in rooms that need a credible AI voice with actual field experience: building BC's AI community infrastructure, training creative and organizational teams, photographing cultural history, hosting meetups, and turning keynotes into durable public resources.
