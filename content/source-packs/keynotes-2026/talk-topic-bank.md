# Talk Topic Bank

These are public-safe talk cards for the 2026 Speaking page and related Work/About cross-links.

## Signature Keynotes

### Both Hands Full

**Positioning:** A keynote for filmmakers, artists, leaders, and organizations trying to stay human while AI gets very good at making everything.

**Promise:** Audiences leave with a practical frame for holding critique and curiosity together: one hand on the real risks, one hand on the creative possibilities, both feet still moving.

**Best for:** Festivals, creative conferences, leadership offsites, higher education, cultural organizations, AI literacy programs.

**Public proof:** World AI Film Festival Brasil 2026 / `https://www.bothhandsfull.com/`

### Punk Rock AI / Creative Rebellion

**Positioning:** A maker-first keynote about agency, taste, creative sovereignty, and using AI without surrendering the weird human parts that make culture matter.

**Promise:** Audiences get beyond tool demos into creative posture: what to automate, what to protect, what to train, what to refuse, and how to build a creative corpus that sounds like them.

**Best for:** CreativeMornings-style communities, design teams, creative agencies, schools, arts organizations, innovation events.

**Public proof:** CreativeMornings Vancouver portal / `https://www.punkrockai.com/`

### Developing an AI Mindset

**Positioning:** A practical keynote/workshop for teams that need shared language, confidence, and curiosity before AI becomes another forced software rollout.

**Promise:** Audiences learn how to approach AI as collaborator, mentor, muse, research assistant, and workflow partner while keeping human judgment at the center.

**Best for:** Associations, companies, schools, nonprofits, creative teams, and professional groups starting their AI journey.

**Public proof:** `http://developinganaimindset.com/`

### Compost AI

**Positioning:** A talk/workshop about turning archives, decisions, notes, stories, and relationships into sovereign creative soil instead of extractive data exhaust.

**Promise:** Audiences learn how to build personal or organizational memory systems that respect consent, preserve context, and make future work more alive.

**Best for:** Knowledge teams, artists, publishers, cultural organizations, civic groups, and communities with rich archives.

**Status:** Available topic. Treat as in development until a public delivered-event source is verified.

### Leadership After the AI Point of No Return

**Positioning:** A leadership keynote for organizations that cannot wait for the AI dust to settle.

**Promise:** Leaders get a humane operating frame: ship useful experiments, hold the line on trust and consent, and build governance that people will actually use.

**Best for:** Executive audiences, associations, public sector groups, higher education, boards, and leadership retreats.

**Status:** Available topic. Use as a program option, not as past-stage proof.

### Power, Taste, and Trust

**Positioning:** A keynote on human judgment in the age of invisible AI infrastructure.

**Promise:** Audiences learn why taste, trust, and power become the strategic layer when models, tools, and automation become cheap and everywhere.

**Best for:** Product leaders, design teams, policy audiences, founders, and organizations building AI-enabled services.

**Status:** Available topic. Use as a program option, not as past-stage proof.
