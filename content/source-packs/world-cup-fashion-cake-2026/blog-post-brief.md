## Excerpt

An old fashion-industry collaboration gets rebooted through mood boards, Midjourney, Lightroom, motion tests, and a small army of AI tools, turning Vancouver's 2026 World Cup energy into an avant-garde photo essay with hairstylist Tania Becker.

## Angle

This is not a generic "AI made some fashion images" article. It is a first-person field note about friendship, taste, creative process, and the strange usefulness of explaining new tools to someone brilliant enough to run with them.

Core thesis:

> AI does not replace the eye. It makes the eye busier, faster, weirder, and more responsible.

## Outline

### 1. The Old Fashion Signal

Open with Kris and Tania's fashion-industry history: photographer and hairstylist, taste formed in real shoots, real sets, real bodies, real light.

### 2. Becker's Eye

Introduce Tania as a high-end LA-based avant-garde fashion hairstylist. Focus on sculptural hair, editorial nerve, and the kind of visual taste that makes AI tools useful.

### 3. The Workflow

Explain the practical loop:

1. Save reference images.
2. Build a Midjourney mood board.
3. Talk the idea out.
4. Generate prompt batches.
5. Run rough generations.
6. Pull outputs into a selection/editing environment.
7. Make top selects.
8. Test motion only after the still image has promise.

### 4. Vancouver 2026 As A Visual Engine

Use Vancouver World Cup 2026 as the cultural pressure: BC Place, seven match days, rain, stadium lights, hotel lobbies, train platforms, mountain fog, and the feeling of the city becoming an international stage.

### 5. Lost Angels Meets Vancouver

Frame the aesthetic collision: LA fashion polish, Vancouver weather, Pacific light, soccer-coded couture, and Becker hair.

### 6. The Army Of Robots

Reflect on GPT/Claude/Midjourney/Lightroom and the broader toolchain. Keep the point human: the tools accelerate iteration, but judgment still lives with the collaborators.

### 7. What Comes Next

Point readers to the microsite/photo essay once live. Note that final images are curated selects, not raw first passes.
